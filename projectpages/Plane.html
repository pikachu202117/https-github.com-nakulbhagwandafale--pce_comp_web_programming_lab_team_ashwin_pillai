Are you ready to Make a PLANE LAUNCHER with ADDS!!!

Here is the list of components you will be needing.

  1. dc hobby motor (2)
  2. wire leads (4)
  3. alligator clips (4)
  4. battery pack (2)
  5. aa batteries (4)
  6. wooden wheel (2) - 2" diameter
  7. rubber band (2) - flat
  8. hot glue / hot glue gun
  9. container lid - plastic
  10. pushpins
  11. Markers
  12. wooden base (1) - 3/4" x 5 1/2" x 16 1/2"
  13. wooden blocks (2) - 2” x 1” x ¾”
  14. wooden poles (4) - 3 ¾” x ⅜” x ⅜”
  15. wooden runway (2) - 12”x 1 ½” x ¼”
  16. paint & paint brush - yellow and black

simply follow are easy steps to build your own project.so here we goo!!

  • step 1: Hot glue a flat rubber band around the outside of a wooden wheel. Repeat for the second wheel.

  • step 2: Create a small plastic circle using the pushpin. Push the pushpin into the lid and trace around it with a marker. Repeat again to make a second circle.

  • step 3: Cut out one of the small circles and hot glue it to the center of the wooden wheel. Repeat for the other wheel

  • step 4: Connect the battery leads to the loose wire leads.

  • step 5:Connect the alligator clips to the free end of the leads

  • step 6: Place batteries in the battery holders. Then, close them.

  • step 7: Connect the free ends of the alligator clips to the motor as seen in the photo.

  • step 8: Test the motors! Place each wheel onto the top of either motor and turn the battery holders on. Make sure that the wheels are spinning in opposite directions.

  • step 9:Disconnect the motors from the alligator clips. Then, hot glue the motors to the smallest wooden blocks so that the motors are flush to the top of the blocks.

  • step 10: Fill the hole in the center of the wheel with hot glue so that it is as stable as possible. Scrape any excess hot glue off the top with a scrap piece of paper or cardboard.

  • step 11: Mark the base ½” from either short side and 4 ¼” from the bottom. Then, hot glue the mounted motors on your mark. Note: The bottom is the end of the base closest to the mounted motors.

  • step 12:Mark the base ¼” from either short side and 2 ¾” from the bottom. Also, mark the base ¼” from either short side and 3 ¼” from the top. Then, hot glue the the wooden poles on your marks

  • step 13: Paint the wooden runways so that they look like a real runway or design them however you like! This is where your plane will “take off” from.

  • step 14: Hot glue the runways to the poles. Note: Make sure to leave about ¼” of space between the wheels and the runway.

  • step 15: Connect the free ends of the alligator clips to the motors. Then, test them to make sure that the wheels are spinning in opposite directions.

  • step 16: Now you're ready to launch some paper airplanes! Load a paper airplane into your launcher and watch it fly away. Try angling your launcher by placing some books underneath it and see what happens!

And thats it, here you are ready with your project.
