Are you ready to build The Night Sky Lamp with ADDS!!!

Here is the list of components you will be needing.

  1. Tin can
  2. Nails
  3. Hammer
  4. Spray paint
  5. Permanent marker
  6. Fairy lights
  7. Scotch tape
  8. Cardboard
  9. Scissors
  10. Multipurpose glue

simply follow are easy steps to build your own project.so here we goo!!

  • step 1: Press the sharp edgesUsing the plier, press the edges of the tin can to pinch down the sharp edges of the metal.

  • step 2: Draw the constellations.
    On each side of the tin can mark the holes where the stars of the constellations will be at. You can print them to make sure you'll put each star in the right position .

  • step 3: Pierce the stars at the tin can,
    With the thicker nail, pierce a hole on each mark of the constellations. Then, with the thinner nail, pierce other stars randomly around the can. Make as many stars as you want.

  • step 4: Paint the constellation projector.
    Choose your favourite colour to paint the tin can. I opted for the black paint to make it look like a night sky.

  • step 5: Draw the constellations.
    After the paint is dry, using the permanent marker, draw the lines connecting the stars of the constellations. If you wish, you can also write their names next to them.

  • step 6: Cut the cardboard.
    Place the tin can over the cardboard and trace around it. Cut the cardboard and make a small gap with the scissors to fit the fairy light plug.

  • step 7: Insert the fairy lights Stick the fairy light batteries to the cardboard making sure the plug is in the hole. Then, place the fairy lights inside the stars projector.

  • step 8: Close the bottom of the stars projector.
    Place the cardboard over the tin can. Glue the cardboard to the tin with tape. I'm using black insulating tape to match the colour I've painted the tin can.

  • step 9: Turn the kid's night light on
    Turn it on and watch your homemade night light projector for kids come to life.

And thats it, here you are ready with your project.
